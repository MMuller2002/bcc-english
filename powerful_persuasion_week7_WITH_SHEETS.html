Key concepts | 8-23 minFive Delivery Techniques + Script Notation
8-23 minv

Copy the notation system. Tap each card to reveal when each technique is most powerful.

VOLUME

How loud or quiet you speak. Contrast matters more than absolute level.

LOUD / soft Tap to reveal when to use it v Quieter for intimate, personal moments -- it pulls the audience in. Louder for key arguments and the call to action. Never the same volume throughout.

PACE

How fast or slow you speak. Slowing down signals importance.

SLOW / FAST Tap to reveal when to use it v Slow for your contention, your key statistics, and your call to action. Context and examples can move faster. Never rush your most important sentences.

PITCH

High or low tone of voice. Rise for questions, fall for authority.

RISE / FALL Tap to reveal when to use it v Falling pitch at the end of a statement creates authority. Rising pitch builds tension and curiosity. Avoid upward inflection on every sentence -- it sounds uncertain.

PAUSE

Intentional silence before or after a key moment.

// (pause) Tap to reveal when to use it v After your hook -- let it land. Before your contention -- make the audience wait for it. After your call to action -- let the silence be the final word. Three seconds feels enormous. Use it.

EMPHASIS

Stressing a specific word to control meaning and create impact.

STRESS or underline Tap to reveal when to use it v On statistics, on your key claim word, on your call to action verb. Say "The evidence is UNAMBIGUOUS" not "The EVIDENCE is unambiguous" -- stress what you want to land.

Eye contactLook up at the hook, contention, and call to action. Aim for 70% eye contact.Reading the entire speech from paper without looking up.
PostureStand tall, feet shoulder-width apart. Grounded posture signals confidence.Slumping, shifting weight, or gripping the lectern.
